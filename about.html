Application Notes:

1. To add a 10 hour break, input the miles you would like to calculate,
and then press "Get ETA". Click on either the slider, or the text that
says "Calculate for 10 hour break". The ETA will then be displayed bellow.

2. The store function is useful when trying to calculate multiple ETA's,
for expample: If you need to know the time a driver will arrive at a fuel stop
and then also at a destination after.

3. ETA's are calculated by dividing the miles from point a to b by
55mph. All times are generated for the central time zone by default.
